## Headline

### OpenAI Closes $122B Funding Round at $852B Valuation — The AI Infrastructure Race Enters a New Phase

**What happened**: On March 31, OpenAI announced the close of its latest funding round with $122 billion in committed capital at a post-money valuation of $852 billion — the largest single funding round in tech history.

**Key metrics**:
- Monthly revenue has reached $2B ($24B annualized)
- ChatGPT has 900 million weekly active users, 50 million paid subscribers
- Enterprise revenue now exceeds 40% of total, on track to reach parity with consumer by end of 2026
- Codex serves 2M+ weekly users, up 5x in three months
- APIs process 15B+ tokens per minute

**Investor lineup**:
- Strategic anchors: Amazon, NVIDIA, SoftBank
- Long-term partner: Microsoft
- Co-leads: a16z, D. E. Shaw Ventures, MGX, TPG, T. Rowe Price
- Participants: BlackRock, Blackstone, Sequoia, Temasek, Thrive Capital, and others
- First-time bank channel offering raised $3B+ from individual investors
- Revolving credit facility expanded to ~$4.7B (JPMorgan, Goldman Sachs syndicate, undrawn)

**Infrastructure strategy**:
OpenAI is explicitly treating infrastructure as a "strategic moat," building a diversified stack across multiple clouds, chips, and data centers:
- Cloud: Microsoft, Oracle, AWS, CoreWeave, Google Cloud
- Silicon: NVIDIA, AMD, AWS Trainium, Cerebras, custom chip (with Broadcom)
- Data centers: Oracle, SBE, SoftBank partnerships

**Superapp vision**: OpenAI announced plans for a "unified AI superapp" combining ChatGPT, Codex, search, and agentic capabilities into a single agent-first experience.

The sheer scale of this round is staggering — $122B is comparable to the GDP of many countries. But the signal matters more than the number: the AI infrastructure race has escalated from "who can train better models" to "who controls the entire compute-model-product-distribution flywheel." OpenAI's strategy is clear — use consumer scale to drive enterprise penetration, then use enterprise revenue to fund more infrastructure. The ARK Invest ETF inclusion means retail investors can now get indirect exposure to OpenAI for the first time — a milestone for AI industry capitalization.

## Anthropic

### Research

#### How Australia Uses Claude: Anthropic Economic Index Report

Anthropic released a deep-dive analysis of Claude usage in Australia, part of its Economic Index series. The report coincides with Anthropic opening a new Sydney office and signing an AI safety research MOU with the Australian government.

**Key findings**:
- Australia accounts for 1.6% of global Claude.ai traffic, ranking 11th worldwide
- Per capita usage is 4.1x expected, ranking 7th globally (behind Singapore, Israel, Luxembourg, Switzerland, US, Canada)
- Usage concentrated in New South Wales (37%) and Victoria (31%)
- 46% work, 47% personal, 7% coursework
- Australian users tend to ask more complex questions (higher education level required) but tasks take ~20% less time than global average
- AI autonomy score: 3.38/5 — Australians prefer collaborative, less delegated usage

Notably, state-level income doesn't predict Claude adoption — mining-heavy Western Australia has the highest GDP per capita but low usage. This reinforces that AI adoption is driven more by industry structure than economic output.

**Link**: [Original](https://www.anthropic.com/research/how-australia-uses-claude)

## NVIDIA

### RTX AI Garage: Gemma 4 Optimized for Local Deployment on NVIDIA Hardware

NVIDIA published a technical blog detailing Gemma 4's optimized deployment across RTX GPUs, DGX Spark, and Jetson Orin Nano.

**Highlights**:
- Co-optimized with Google for inference performance across the full Gemma 4 family
- 31B model runs efficiently on RTX 5090
- E2B/E4B run with near-zero latency on Jetson Nano edge devices
- Supports Ollama, llama.cpp, Unsloth, and other mainstream local deployment tools
- Compatible with OpenClaw and other local agent platforms

NVIDIA is aggressively pushing an "AI everywhere" strategy — from data centers to desktops to edge devices. Gemma 4's Apache 2.0 license combined with NVIDIA's hardware optimization means developers can deploy frontier AI capabilities locally without relying on cloud APIs. For privacy-conscious enterprises and developers, this is a significant inflection point.

**Link**: [Original](https://blogs.nvidia.com/blog/rtx-ai-garage-open-models-google-gemma-4/)

## Trend Watch

**AI Capital Arms Race Heats Up**: OpenAI's $122B round raises the capital bar to an entirely new level. This money isn't just for training models — it's for building end-to-end vertical integration from chips to data centers to products to distribution. The space left for "small and beautiful" AI companies is shrinking.

**Open Source AI's Usability Revolution**: Gemma 4's Apache 2.0 license combined with NVIDIA's full-stack optimization marks a qualitative shift from "can run" to "runs great." When a 31B model runs smoothly on consumer GPUs, the practical gap between open and closed models becomes very small indeed.

**AI's Geographic Map Redrawn**: Google's $15B India investment, Anthropic's Sydney expansion, and government AI skills programs worldwide signal that AI's main battlefield is expanding beyond Silicon Valley. APAC, with its population scale and digital adoption speed, is becoming AI's proving ground.

**AI Safety Enters the Psychology Era**: Anthropic's emotion concepts research opens a new door. If AI models truly make decisions through functional emotion mechanisms, traditional "alignment" methods may not suffice — we may need behavioral science approaches to understand and shape AI behavior.
