## Top Story

### Anthropic Economic Index: Learning Curves and the Matthew Effect of AI Adoption

**What happened**: Anthropic released its March 2026 Economic Index report, analyzing AI's economic impact based on Claude usage data from February 2026.

**Key findings**:

1. **Diversification of use cases**: The top 10 tasks on Claude.ai dropped from 24% of conversations (November 2025) to 19%—users are expanding beyond coding into sports queries, product comparisons, and home maintenance.

2. **Average task value decreased**: From $49.3/hour to $47.9/hour. This isn't bad—it's the classic technology diffusion curve: early adopters focus on high-value coding tasks; later adopters bring AI into everyday work.

3. **Learning curves are real**: Users with 6+ months on Claude have 10% higher conversation success rates than newcomers—and this gap isn't explained by task selection or geography. Anthropic calls this evidence of "learning-by-doing."

4. **Geographic divide persists**: Globally, the top 20 countries by per-capita usage now account for 48% of total usage, up from 45%. The gap is widening, not closing.

**Why it matters**:

This report provides the first quantitative evidence of "AI adoption inequality." If high-usage regions/users gain more productivity, and these gains self-reinforce (because they become better at using AI), we may be witnessing a new Matthew Effect—the rich get richer.

For policymakers, this means that simply promoting AI access isn't enough. Skills training and usage guidance are essential complements.

## OpenAI

### Inside the Model Spec: AI's "Constitution"

**What happened**: OpenAI published a detailed explanation of the Model Spec—the formal framework defining how its models should behave, essentially AI's "constitution."

**Key points**:

- **Chain of Command**: When OpenAI policies, developer instructions, and user requests conflict, how does the model decide? The Model Spec defines clear priorities.
- **Hard rules vs. defaults**: True "red lines" are few (e.g., no weapons instructions); most behaviors are adjustable defaults that developers/users can modify.
- **Transparency as accountability**: Publishing the Model Spec enables public scrutiny, critique, and improvement of model behavior norms.

**Analysis**: The Model Spec is OpenAI's clearest behavioral statement yet. But it's fundamentally an "intent declaration," not a "capability guarantee"—models won't always perfectly follow it. The real question: when models violate the Model Spec, who's accountable?

**Link**: [Original](https://openai.com/index/our-approach-to-the-model-spec/)

### Safety Bug Bounty Program Launches

**What happened**: OpenAI launched a Safety Bug Bounty program inviting researchers to find abuse and safety risks in AI systems.

**Scope**:
- Agent risks (prompt injection and data exfiltration via MCP)
- Proprietary information leakage
- Account and platform integrity vulnerabilities

**Out of scope**: Generic jailbreaks without demonstrable harm.

**Analysis**: This acknowledges the security reality of the agent era. When AI can browse the web, execute code, and call APIs, prompt injection isn't just about making models say bad words—it can lead to data breaches or malicious actions.

**Link**: [Original](https://openai.com/index/safety-bug-bounty/)

## Google DeepMind

### AGI Cognitive Framework: Measuring General Intelligence

**What happened**: DeepMind released "Measuring Progress Toward AGI: A Cognitive Taxonomy," proposing a cognitive science-based framework for evaluating AI capabilities, alongside a $200,000 Kaggle hackathon.

**The 10 cognitive abilities**:
Perception, Generation, Attention, Learning, Memory, Reasoning, Metacognition, Executive Functions, Problem Solving, Social Cognition

**Evaluation approach**:
1. Test AI systems across cognitive task batteries
2. Collect human baselines from representative adult samples
3. Map AI performance relative to human performance distributions

**Hackathon focus**: Learning, metacognition, attention, executive functions, social cognition—the areas with the largest evaluation gaps.

**Analysis**: DeepMind is "normalizing" AGI discourse through cognitive science. Smart move—instead of debating "what is AGI," build measurable capability dimensions. But cognitive science has limits: human intelligence may not be the only or best reference for general intelligence.

This week's theme is **measurability**:
