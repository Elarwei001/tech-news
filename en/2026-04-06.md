## Headline

### OpenAI Shuts Down Sora App: A Cautionary Tale for AI Video Generation

On March 25, OpenAI announced on X that it was discontinuing the Sora app and API — less than six months after the launch of Sora 2 and its standalone app. The app will shut down on April 26, and the API on September 24, 2026.

**Timeline**:
- **Feb 2024**: Sora unveiled, stunning the world
- **Sep 2025**: Sora 2 launched; standalone app hit #1 on Apple's App Store within days
- **Mar 24, 2026**: OpenAI published "Creating with Sora safely," detailing C2PA metadata, dynamic watermarks, teen protections
- **Mar 25, 2026**: Shutdown announced

**Why Sora was shelved**:
1. **Persistent safety controversies**: Criticism over violent and racist content, celebrity deepfakes, and misuse of copyrighted characters
2. **Strategic pivot**: An OpenAI spokesperson said the company is focusing on "robotics and coding function designed to help people solve real-world physical tasks"
3. **IPO preparation**: OpenAI may go public as early as Q4 2026, and is refocusing on enterprise and developer products

**Impact**:
- Disney content partnership (3-year deal covering 200+ licensed characters) terminated
- Sora 2 model remains available through ChatGPT Plus ($20/month), but standalone social features are gone
- Competitors like Kling AI and Google Veo continue operating

**Analysis**: Sora's rise and fall is an extreme case of the AI industry's "move fast" philosophy. The technical quality was impressive, but wrapping a powerful, hard-to-control video generation tool as a social media platform was inherently controversial. Publishing safety measures one day and announcing shutdown the next speaks to the severity of the challenges. More importantly, Sora's closure signals an acceleration of OpenAI's strategic shift from general consumer products to enterprise and developer tools.

## OpenAI

### Record $122B Funding Round, $852B Valuation

On March 31, OpenAI announced a record-breaking $122B funding round (up from the previously announced $110B), led by Amazon, Nvidia, and SoftBank, valuing the company at $852B.

**Key metrics**:
- Enterprise revenue now exceeds 40% of total, on track for parity with consumer by end of 2026
- GPT-5.4 driving growth
- Agentic workflows (Codex etc.) emerging as a growth engine
- $3B from retail investors — unprecedented for a pre-IPO company

### OpenAI Foundation: $1B+ First-Year Investment

On March 24, the OpenAI Foundation announced its first major deployment plan: investing at least $1B over the next year across four pillars:

1. **Life Sciences & Curing Diseases** (part of a previously announced $25B commitment):
   - AI for Alzheimer's: disease pathway mapping, biomarker detection, personalized treatment

   - High-mortality diseases: accelerating therapy development
   - Led by Jacob Trefethen (formerly Coefficient Giving)

2. **Jobs & Economic Impact**: Research on how AI changes work
3. **AI Resilience**: Helping society adapt to advanced AI
4. **Community Programs**: Local support

### Safety Bug Bounty Program Launches

OpenAI launched a dedicated [Safety Bug Bounty](https://bugcrowd.com/engagements/openai-safety) complementing the existing Security Bug Bounty. Focus areas:

- **Agentic risks (including MCP)**: Third-party prompt injection and data exfiltration (requires 50%+ reproducibility), large-scale disallowed actions
- **OpenAI proprietary information**: Model reasoning information leakage
- **Account & platform integrity**: Bypassing anti-automation controls, manipulating trust signals

Note: General jailbreaks are out of scope, but private bug bounty campaigns targeting specific harm categories (e.g., Biorisk) run periodically.

**Links**: [Original post](https://openai.com/index/safety-bug-bounty/)

### Open-Source Teen Safety Policies

OpenAI released a prompt-based [safety policy pack](https://github.com/openai/teen-safety-policy-pack) designed for the open-weight safety model gpt-oss-safeguard. Covers six risk areas: graphic violent content, graphic sexual content, harmful body ideals, dangerous activities, romantic/violent roleplay, and age-restricted goods/services.

Policies are structured as prompts that work directly with gpt-oss-safeguard and other reasoning models, supporting real-time content filtering and offline analysis. Developed with Common Sense Media and everyone.ai.

**Links**: [Original post](https://openai.com/index/teen-safety-policies-gpt-oss-safeguard/)

### ChatGPT Shopping Product Discovery Upgrade

OpenAI expanded the Agentic Commerce Protocol (ACP) to support product discovery. Users can visually browse products based on budget and preferences, compare side-by-side, and upload images for similar items. Shopify merchant data auto-integrates via Shopify Catalog. Walmart launched an in-ChatGPT app experience.

Notably, OpenAI abandoned Instant Checkout, letting merchants use their own checkout experiences while focusing on product discovery.

**Links**: [Original post](https://openai.com/index/powering-product-discovery-in-chatgpt/)

## Anthropic

### Economic Index: "Learning Curves" Report Reveals Experience Effects in AI Usage

On March 24, Anthropic released the latest [Economic Index report](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-march-2026-report), focusing on learning curves — how users improve their AI usage through experience.

**Key Findings**:

1. **Diversification of use cases**: Claude.ai's top 10 tasks dropped from 24% (Nov 2025) to 19% (Feb 2026). Coding tasks migrating from Claude.ai to API (Claude Code). Personal use rose from 35% to 42%.

2. **"Learning curve" effect**:
   - Users with 6+ months tenure: 10% fewer personal conversations, 6% higher education level in inputs
   - Key finding: higher-tenure users have a **10% higher success rate** — unexplained by task selection, country, or other factors
   - Possible evidence of "learning-by-doing": people get better at using Claude through practice

3. **Model selection matches task complexity**: Paying users prefer Opus for coding tasks (+4pp vs average) and lighter models for tutoring (-7pp). This effect is twice as strong among API users.

4. **Persistent global inequality**: Top 20 countries account for 48% of global per-capita usage (up from 45%). But US internal convergence: Top 10 states' share dropped from 40% to 38%.

5. **Broad penetration**: ~49% of occupations have at least a quarter of their tasks performed using Claude.

**Links**: [Report](https://www.anthropic.com/research/economic-index-march-2026-report) | [Full PDF](https://cdn.sanity.io/files/4zrzovbb/website/f065d6e6f92c65df8244042c83d48872ea308c3a.pdf)

### Global AI Attitudes Survey: 81,000 People, 159 Countries, 70 Languages

On March 18, Anthropic released an unprecedented global qualitative study: inviting Claude.ai users to share how they use AI, what they dream it could enable, and what they fear it might do.

**Scale**: 80,508 interviews across 159 countries and 70 languages, using the "Anthropic Interviewer" for structured interviews — the largest and most multilingual qualitative study of its kind.

**Key findings**:
- Respondents prioritize practical needs: professional excellence (18.8%), personal growth and practical assistance
- Fears center on AI losing control, job displacement, and privacy violations

## Trends

### Three Themes This Week

1. **OpenAI's Strategic Contraction & Focus**: Shutting down Sora, dropping Instant Checkout, launching a Safety Bug Bounty — OpenAI is doing subtraction. Ahead of a $122B raise and potential IPO, the company is clearing business lines and concentrating resources on high-growth, high-certainty enterprise products and developer tools. Sora's lesson: consumer AI needs more mature safety and business models.

2. **"AI Literacy" is a Learnable Skill**: Anthropic's "learning curves" report may be the most important long-term insight of the week. The 10% success rate gap suggests AI's value depends not just on model capability but on user capability. This means AI skills training could become a significant topic in labor economics.

3. **Engineering Safety Infrastructure**: From OpenAI's Safety Bug Bounty and teen safety policy pack to Sora's C2PA/watermark scheme, AI safety is shifting from "principled statements" to "actionable engineering tools." Encoding safety policies as prompts, crowdsourcing safety testing through bug bounties — these practices are turning AI safety from an aspiration into an engineering discipline.
