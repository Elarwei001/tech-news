## Headline

### Mythos Aftershocks: Global Regulators Scramble to Respond to Anthropic's Cybersecurity AI Model

**Background**: Anthropic released Claude Mythos Preview on April 7-8 — a general-purpose frontier model demonstrating unprecedented cybersecurity capabilities. The model autonomously discovered thousands of zero-day vulnerabilities across every major operating system and browser, including a 27-year-old OpenBSD bug and a 16-year-old FFmpeg vulnerability. Rather than a public release, Anthropic launched Project Glasswing, partnering with 11 tech giants (AWS, Apple, Broadcom, Cisco, CrowdStrike, Google, JPMorganChase, Linux Foundation, Microsoft, NVIDIA, Palo Alto Networks) and committing $100M in usage credits for defensive security work.

**This Week's Regulatory Response**:

**United States** (April 10, CNBC): Vice President JD Vance and Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent urgently questioned leading tech CEOs — including Anthropic's Dario Amodei, Google's Sundar Pichai, OpenAI's Sam Altman, and Microsoft's Satya Nadella — about AI model security and cyberattack response, one week before Anthropic released Mythos. That same day, Bessent and Fed Chair Jerome Powell summoned bank CEOs to the Treasury for an emergency briefing on Mythos-related cybersecurity risks.

**United Kingdom** (April 12, Financial Times): The Bank of England, Financial Conduct Authority, and HM Treasury are in urgent talks with the National Cyber Security Centre to assess vulnerabilities in critical IT systems highlighted by Anthropic's model. Representatives from major British banks, insurers, and exchanges will be briefed by regulators within the next fortnight.

**Canada**: Canadian financial regulators are also following up to assess cyber risks from the Mythos model, per Washington Today.

**Analysis**:

This is a watershed moment in AI regulation. Regulators are no longer dealing with abstract "AI risk" discussions — they're confronting a specific model that has already proven it can autonomously break into the world's most secure systems. This urgency is entirely new.

Several dimensions stand out:

1. **Financial sector as the first line of defense**: Both US and UK regulatory actions targeted financial institutions first, not tech companies. This reflects a recognition that if AI-augmented cyberattacks materialize, financial systems are both the most vulnerable and most critical targets.

2. **Bipartisan consensus**: The Vance/Bessent actions demonstrate that cybersecurity threats are among the least politically divisive issues in Washington, regardless of ideology.

3. **Anthropic's strategy is working**: By proactively restricting release and partnering with industry leaders, Anthropic avoided "irresponsible release" criticism while shaping the narrative around defense rather than threats. Project Glasswing's $100M commitment isn't charity — it's a shrewd strategy to convert a potential regulatory crisis into an industry collaboration opportunity.

4. **Emerging global coordination**: The near-simultaneous regulatory responses across the US, UK, and Canada suggest some degree of information sharing and policy coordination. This could be the genesis of a global cybersecurity governance framework for the AI era.

The deepest question remains unanswered: when Mythos-class capabilities inevitably proliferate to more actors (including malicious ones), can current defense systems hold? Project Glasswing's 90-day public reporting window will be a critical observation period.

**Technical Details** (from [Anthropic Frontier Red Team blog](https://red.anthropic.com/2026/mythos-preview/)):

Mythos Preview's security testing used a simple agentic scaffold — launching an isolated container with the target project's source code, driving Claude Code with the Mythos Preview model, and prompting it to "find a security vulnerability in this program." A key design choice: each run focused on a different file, with the model first ranking files by vulnerability likelihood (1-5 scale) and starting from the highest priority.

Cost breakdown: the specific run that found the 27-year OpenBSD bug cost under $50, but the full thousand-run campaign (discovering dozens of vulnerabilities) totaled approximately $20,000. FFmpeg-related vulnerability discoveries cost roughly $10,000.

## OpenAI

### New $100/Month ChatGPT Pro Tier, Targeting Claude Max

**What happened**: OpenAI launched a new ChatGPT Pro tier on April 9 at $100/month, filling the gap between the $20 Plus and $200 Pro plans. The new tier offers 5x Codex usage compared to Plus, with a promotional 10x multiplier through May.

**Key details**:
- **GPT-5.4-mini**: 1,200–7,000 local messages every 5 hours
- **GPT-5.3-Codex**: 600–3,000 local messages and 200–1,200 cloud tasks every 5 hours
- **Code Reviews**: 400–1,000 every 5 hours
- **Target audience**: Heavy developers, especially Codex power users

**Context**: This move is a direct response to Anthropic's Claude Max ($100/month). Reports indicate an OpenAI executive told her team to stop "side quests" and focus on competing with Anthropic and Claude Code. Both companies are reportedly planning IPOs this year.
